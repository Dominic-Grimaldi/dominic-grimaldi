HUD Proposes Law Change

This publication brings awareness to the reinstatement of 2013 fair housing guidelines to combat discrimination and support unhoused individuals, especially considering those with conviction histories. The rollback provided more stable housing opportunities to former convicts and attempts to reduce homelessness.

Law SB4

I wrote this piece to highlight the importance and impact of California's recently passed SB4 law, which addresses the state's critical low-income housing shortage. The law simplifies and expedites the process for nonprofit organizations, particularly faith-based institutions, to rezone unused land for affordable housing projects. This is a crucial step toward mitigating homelessness, as these projects were often delayed by the lengthy and costly approval processes imposed by the California Environmental Quality Act (CEQA).

New Jersey Homeless Bill of Rights

New Jersey is considering a bill of rights for the homeless to prevent discrimination based on housing status, including rights to use public spaces, receive equal treatment by government agencies, seek employment, vote, and access emergency medical care. This initiative, introduced by Senator Britnee M. Timberlake, aims to protect unhoused individuals, especially in light of the potential impact of the Grants Pass case, which might allow cities to clear tent encampments regardless of shelter availability. Advocates warn that criminalizing homelessness exacerbates the problem and contradicts the Eighth Amendment's protection against cruel and unusual punishment.

Victory for the Unhoused

I wrote this piece to highlight the significant legal victory for unhoused individuals in Ontario, where Justice Robert Centra struck down most of the Safe Streets Act, a law that severely restricted panhandling. By declaring the act unconstitutional, Centra reinforced the importance of freedom of expression and the social value of soliciting charity, even if it makes some people uncomfortable. This decision sets a crucial precedent, emphasizing that penalizing unhoused individuals only exacerbates their struggles without addressing the root causes of homelessness.
